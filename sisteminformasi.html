Information Systems Study Program

The Information Systems major is a study program that emphasizes the use of information technology to manage, analyze, and utilize information in the context of organizations and businesses. The main objective of this major is to prepare students to develop, manage, and improve effective and efficient information systems to support business operations and decision making. During their studies, students will learn the basics of information technology, such as computer programming, databases, computer networks, systems analysis, and software design. Students will also learn about IT project management, information security, e-business, and IT strategy in business. In addition, the Information Systems major also often involves practical experience through internships in information technology companies, corporate IT departments, or government agencies related to IT to apply the knowledge and skills they learn in real-world situations. Later, graduates from this major have broad and diverse career opportunities, including as system analysts, software developers, database administrators, IT managers, or IT business consultants.

Academic Information

  • Curriculum: A structured curriculum covering database management, systems analysis and design, network technologies, cybersecurity, and business process modeling.
  • Specialization or Concentration: Potential specializations may include Business Intelligence, Data Analytics, or Cybersecurity within Information Systems.
  • Teaching Method: A blend of lectures, hands-on lab sessions, case studies, and project-based learning to ensure practical application of theoretical knowledge.

Graduate Profile

  • Core competencies: Graduates will possess expertise in designing, developing, implementing, and managing information systems, along with strong analytical and problem-solving skills.
  • Career opportunities: System Analyst, Database Administrator, Business Intelligence Analyst, IT Project Manager, Cybersecurity Analyst.
  • Additional skills: Strong communication, teamwork, and leadership skills, along with an understanding of business processes and organizational strategy.

Accreditation

The Information Systems Study Program has received Good accreditation from the National Accreditation Board for Higher Education (BAN-PT).
